What Our Scholarship Recipients Say

It serves as a lasting tribute to his contributions and spirit, celebrating those who exemplify the qualities he demonstrated in their academic or personal endeavors.

The bpTT Brighter Prospects Programme has truly been a beacon of hope in my academic, personal, and professional journey. When I was denied GATE funding, the dream of pursuing my degree seemed almost out of reach. With only my mother the sole provider for our household working tirelessly to support us, paying out of pocket for my education felt overwhelming. Yet, I was determined not to give up. In the first semester of my studies, I faced an even greater personal challenge, the passing of my father on December 20th,

2022, right in the middle of my exams. Coping with grief while balancing coursework tested my resilience in ways I had never experienced before. It was during these moments that the Brighter Prospects Programme made all the difference offering not just financial assistance, but emotional support, mentorship, and a network of people who genuinely believed in my potential.

I am currently pursuing a degree in Hospitality and Tourism Management, a field that perfectly aligns with my passion for service, culture, and community development. My goal is to use my education to bring greater awareness to the natural beauty and rich heritage of my hometown, Mayaro. I envision creating initiatives that will boost eco-tourism, empower local businesses, and celebrate everything that makes Mayaro so special. Because of the support from bpTT’s Brighter Prospects Programme, I am not only closer to earning my degree, but I am also more confident in my ability to make a meaningful impact in my community. This programme has taught me that no matter the obstacles, with the right support and perseverance, a brighter future is always within reach. I will always be grateful for the role Brighter Prospects has played in my story.

Jason Philbert

USC Programme: BSc Hospitality and Tourism

My name is Karena Dindial and I have been part of the bpTT Brighter Prospects Programme for about 3 years now. In that time, I can say without hesitation that Brighter Prospects has been instrumental in allowing me to pursue my degree in Medicine at the University of the West Indies Cave hill campus. It has not only allowed me to follow my passion for medicine, but it has also alleviated the burden of the high costs of this education on my mother, who is supporting two children in university. Coming from a single-parent household, I’m no stranger to tough situations, so this scholarship has provided me with immense relief as I continue my journey on this demanding but rewarding path. I could never see myself doing anything but Medicine, and can say safely say that so far, it has exceeded my expectations. I plan to specialize in pediatric neurosurgery, an equally intense field of study but one that I am quite looking forward to. Read More Read More

Karena Dindial

The Brighter Prospects scholarship programme is bp Trinidad and Tobago’s signature educational initiative in the Mayaro community. The programme is designed to promote the pursuit of tertiary, technical and vocational education, amongst Mayaro residents, at approved local and regional institutions.
